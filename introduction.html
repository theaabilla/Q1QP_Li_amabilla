INTRODUCTION


There is always a reason for death. Its many reasons range from accidents to health issues. Health issues are always influenced by one's environment and lifestyle, and it might also be caused by fertility rate.


In this paper, we will be discussing the effect of fertility rate on the life expectancy of people from countries in Europe and Central Asia. We aim to figure out whether the amount of children that you give birth to causes a low life expectancy or a high life expectancy. We will be using 3 quantitative data (fertility rate, male life expectancy, and female life expectancy) and 2 qualitative data (Population and Countries used in Asia and Europe) in order for us to conclude our results.


We collected our data on the website, World Development Indicators - Google Public Data Explorer. In this website, we only collected the data of Europe and some countries in Asia during 2017. The variables we collected justifies that our project is a sample because we did not include all countries in the presentation.
